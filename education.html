Carleton University Courses

Analysis of Variance and Experimental Design

Single and multifactor analysis of variance, orthogonal contrasts and multiple comparisons, analysis of covariance; nested, crossed and repeated measures designs; completely randomized, randomized block, Latin squares, factorial experiments, related topics.

Bayesian Analysis

theory of Bayesian learning, models for Bayesian analysis and algorithms for Bayesian inference. Prior and posterior distributions and statistical inferences.

Categorical Data Analysis

Analysis of one-way and two-way tables of nominal date; multidimensional contingency tables, log-linear models; tests of symmetry, marginal homogeneity in square tables; incomplete tables; tables with ordered categories; fixed margins, logistic models with binary response; measures of association and agreement; biological applications.

Data Mining

Data visualization; knowledge discovery in datasets; unsupervised learning: clustering algorithms; dimension reduction; supervised learning: pattern recognition, smoothing techniques, classification.

High Dimensional Data Analysis

Mathematical Distance
Dimension Reduction
Singular Value Decomposition and Principal Component Analysis
Multiple Dimensional Scaling Plots
Factor Analysis
Lasso

Probability Theory & Mathematical Statistics

Discrete and continuous distributions, moment-generating functions, marginal and conditional distributions, transformation theory, limiting distributions. Point and interval estimation, sufficient statistics, hypothesis testing, chi-square tests with enumeration data.

Survey Sampling

Basic concepts in sampling from finite populations; simple random sampling; stratified sampling; choice of sampling unit; cluster and systematic sampling; introduction to multistage sampling; ratio estimation; sampling with unequal probabilities and with replacement; replicated sampling; related topics.

Stochastic Processes and Applications

Conditional probability and conditional expectation; Stochastic modeling; discrete time Markov chains including classification of states, stationary and limiting distributions; exponential distribution and the Poisson processes; queueing models; applications to computer systems, operations research and social sciences.

Applied Multivariate Analysis

Selected topics in regression and correlation non-linear models. Multivariate statistical methods, principal components, factor analysis, multivariate analysis of variance, discriminant analysis, canonical correlation, analysis of categorical data.

Time Series and Forecasting

Time series regression. Nonstationary and stationary time series models. Nonseasonal and seasonal time series models. ARIMA (Box-Jenkins) models. Smoothing methods. Parameter estimation, model identification, diagnostic checking. Forecasting techniques.

Regression Analysis

Review of simple and multiple regression with matrices, Gauss-Markov theorem, polynomial regression, indicator variables, residual analysis, weighted least squares, variable selection techniques, nonlinear regression, correlation analysis and autocorrelation.

Linear Programming & Combinatorial Optimization

Systems of linear inequalities, formulation of linear programming problems, geometric method, the simplex method, duality theory, complementary slackness, sensitivity analysis, branch-and-bound method and cutting plane method for integer linear programming, applications and extensions. Network flow problems, network simplex method, max-flow min-cut problem, integral polyhedra, minimum-weight spanning tree problem, maximum matching problem, maximum stable set problem, introduction to approximation algorithms.

Carleton Basketball Data Science Internship

“Michael Armanious is the first ever basketball analytics intern at Carleton. He works with the Ravens women’s basketball team, analysing team data and industry trends to improve performance.”

"It’s every athlete’s dream: to be the best. Mathematics and Statistics master’s student Michael Armanious recently completed an unusual internship to help Carleton’s women’s basketball team do just that. He didn’t even need to set foot in the Raven’s Nest gym to do it. Armanious was the first-ever participant in Carleton’s Basketball Analytics Internship, launched in September 2018 by a Carleton alumnus and his wife. It was a perfect match: both the Carleton alumnus and Armanious were long-time basketball players with a keen interest in Big Data. Armanious wrote a Python script to scrape information from a database of basketball statistics, which he then analyzed to determine such things as which plays were most likely to succeed and which teams posed the biggest threat to the Ravens"
